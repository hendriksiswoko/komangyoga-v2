Garuda Wisnu Kencana: Bali's giant statue, up close

MyBroBali Travel Guide · 5 min read

Garuda Wisnu Kencana (GWK) Cultural Park, in the Bukit Peninsula in South Bali, is home to one of the tallest statues in the world — a 121-meter bronze and copper depiction of the Hindu god Vishnu riding the mythical bird Garuda. It's visible from parts of South Bali on a clear day and has become a landmark in its own right, separate from the temples and beaches most visitors associate with Bali.

What's actually at the park

Beyond the statue itself, GWK is a large cultural park with an open-air amphitheater hosting traditional Balinese dance performances, exhibition halls explaining the statue's decade-long construction, garden areas, and viewpoints across the peninsula toward the ocean. It's considerably bigger than a single photo stop — plan for at least two to three hours if you want to see more than just the statue.

Best time to visit

  • Late afternoon works well — the statue catches good light and the heat has eased off
  • Check the schedule for Kecak or other traditional dance performances if you want to catch one
  • Weekdays are noticeably quieter than weekends and Indonesian public holidays

Good to know

There's a fair amount of walking between the entrance, the statue plaza, and the amphitheater, some of it uphill, so comfortable shoes help. The park also has cafes and shaded rest areas, making it manageable even in Bali's midday heat if you pace yourself.

Getting there

GWK is in the Bukit Peninsula, around 30–45 minutes from Kuta, Seminyak, or Canggu, and slightly closer if you're staying in Nusa Dua or Uluwatu. It's often combined with an Uluwatu Temple and Kecak fire dance visit in the same afternoon, since both are on the same peninsula.
